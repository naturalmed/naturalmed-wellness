Accessing the CMS

The CMS (Content Management System) is the tool you use to manage your website content — articles, seminars and page text — without touching any code.

🌐
CMS address

Open your browser and go to: https://www.naturalmed-wellness.com/admin/

1
Open the CMS

Go to the address above in any browser (Safari, Chrome, Firefox).

2
Click "Sign In with GitHub"

A GitHub window will open asking you to authorise the connection.

3
Click "Authorize naturalmed"

This connects the CMS to your website repository. You only need to do this once.

4
You are in!

You will see three collections on the left: Articles, Seminars, and Page Text.

⏱
After saving, wait ~1 minute

When you save anything in the CMS, the website rebuilds automatically. This takes about 60 seconds. You can check progress at: https://github.com/naturalmed/naturalmed-wellness/actions

Writing Articles

Articles are the main content of your website. Each article you publish automatically appears on the Articles page, in the RSS feed, and generates a newsletter ready to send.

✍️
Write article in CMS

Fill in all the fields and click Save

⚙️
Website rebuilds automatically (~1 minute)

No action needed from you

🌐
Article appears live on the website

en/articles.html is updated with the new card

📧
Newsletter is ready to send

Available at naturalmed-wellness.com/newsletter/[slug].html

How to create a new article

1
Click "Articles" in the left menu

You will see a list of all existing articles (or "no entries yet" if this is the first).

2
Click "New" (top right)

A blank form opens with all the fields to fill in.

3
Fill in all the fields

See the field guide below for what to write in each field.

4
Click "Save" (top right)

The article is saved and the website starts rebuilding. Wait ~1 minute.

Article fields guide

FieldWhat to write
Title required The full title of your article, e.g. "Acupuncture and Chronic Pain: A TCM Perspective"
Slug required A short URL-friendly version of the title using only lowercase letters, numbers and hyphens. E.g. acupuncture-and-chronic-pain. No spaces, no accents.
Date required The publication date. Click the calendar icon and select today's date, or the date you want the article to appear.
Category required Select one from the dropdown: Acupuncture, Herbal Medicine, Chinese Dietetics, Qi Theory, Tuina Massage, Women's Health, Mental Wellbeing, or Seasonal Health.
Reading time Estimated minutes to read. A rough guide: count the paragraphs and divide by 3. Default is 8 minutes.
Excerpt required 2–3 sentences summarising the article. This appears in the newsletter email and on the articles listing page. Maximum 250 characters.
Cover image Optional. Click to upload a photo (JPG or PNG, ideally 1200×630 pixels). Can be left empty — a placeholder will be used.
Body required The full article text. Use the toolbar buttons for headings, bold, italic, bullet points and links.
✍️
Writing tips for the Body field
  • Use ## Heading for section titles (click the H2 button in the toolbar)
  • Use **word** for bold text (or click the B button)
  • Use - item for bullet points (or click the list button)
  • Leave a blank line between paragraphs

Managing Seminars

The Seminars section lets you add, edit and remove seminar information that appears on the Seminars page of your website.

1
Click "Seminars" in the left menu

Then click "Seminars page content".

2
Edit the introductory text if needed

The "Page subtitle" and "Intro paragraph" fields control the text at the top of the seminars page.

3
Add a seminar — click "Add seminars list item"

Fill in: Title, Date (optional if TBC), Location, Audience, Description, and Status.

4
Click "Save"

The seminars page rebuilds automatically in ~1 minute.

ℹ️
Status options explained
  • TBC — dates not confirmed yet
  • Announced — dates confirmed, registration not open yet
  • Registration Open — people can register
  • Sold Out — no more places available
  • Completed — seminar has taken place

Editing Page Text

Page Text lets you update key sentences on the Home, About, and Conditions pages without any technical knowledge.

1
Click "Page Text" in the left menu

You will see three sub-sections: About — Biography, Conditions — Introduction, and Home — Key text.

2
Click the section you want to edit

For example, "About — Biography" to update your bio text or pull quote.

3
Edit the text and click "Save"

The page rebuilds in ~1 minute.

SectionWhat you can edit
Home — Key text The subtitle line under the main heading, and the paragraph above the "Book a Consultation" button.
About — Biography Your short bio (shown at the top of the About page), your full biography, and the italic pull quote beside your photo.
Conditions — Introduction The introductory paragraph at the top of the Conditions page.

The Newsletter

Every time you publish a new article, a newsletter is automatically generated and published at a private URL. You then send it to your subscribers directly from Gmail.

📨
Newsletter URL pattern

Each newsletter lives at:
https://www.naturalmed-wellness.com/newsletter/YYYY-MM-slug.html

Example: https://www.naturalmed-wellness.com/newsletter/2026-04-acupuncture-and-chronic-pain.html

The slug in the URL is the same as the one you entered in the article form. The year and month are added automatically from the publication date.

Managing Subscriber Contacts

Your subscribers are stored as a Google Contacts label called "Newsletter NaturalMed". To add a new subscriber:

📖
Google Contacts address

https://contacts.google.com — sign in with naturalmed.wellness@gmail.com

1
Open contacts.google.com

Make sure you are signed in as naturalmed.wellness@gmail.com.

2
Click the "+" button (bottom right) → "Create a contact"

Enter the subscriber's first name, last name, and email address. Click Save.

3
Open the new contact → click the three dots ⋮ → "Label as"

Select "Newsletter NaturalMed". The contact is now in your mailing group.

To remove a subscriber

1
In Google Contacts, click "Newsletter NaturalMed" in the left menu

All your subscribers are listed.

2
Click the three dots ⋮ next to the contact → "Remove from label"

The contact stays in your general contacts but is removed from the newsletter group.

Sending the Newsletter

Once your article is published and the website has rebuilt (~1 minute), follow these steps to send the newsletter to all your subscribers.

1
Open the newsletter page in your browser

The URL is: https://www.naturalmed-wellness.com/newsletter/YYYY-MM-your-slug.html

2
Click "✓ Hide this bar — ready to copy"

This removes the yellow instruction bar so it does not appear in the email.

3
Select all and copy

Press ⌘ Cmd + A to select everything, then ⌘ Cmd + C to copy.

4
Open Gmail and compose a new email

Go to https://mail.google.com and click "Compose".

5
Fill in the "To:" field with your own email

Type naturalmed.wellness@gmail.com in the To field.

6
Click "Bcc" and add the subscriber group

Click the "Bcc" link (top right of the To field). Type Newsletter NaturalMed and select the group when it appears. Using Bcc means subscribers cannot see each other's email addresses.

7
Fill in the Subject line

Use: New article from NaturalMed: [Article Title]

8
Click in the email body and paste

Press ⌘ Cmd + V. The newsletter appears formatted with all colours and images.

9
Click "Send"

All subscribers receive a beautifully formatted newsletter without seeing each other's addresses.

⚠️
Always use Bcc, never To

If you add the group to the "To:" or "Cc:" fields, every subscriber will see the email addresses of all the others. This is a privacy issue. Always add the Newsletter NaturalMed group to Bcc.

Frequently Asked Questions

I saved an article but it is not appearing on the website yet.

Wait 1–2 minutes and refresh the page. The website rebuilds automatically after every save. You can check the progress at https://github.com/naturalmed/naturalmed-wellness/actions — a green tick means it completed successfully.

I want to edit an article I already published.

In the CMS, click "Articles" and click the article you want to edit. Make your changes and click "Save". The website updates automatically.

What is the Slug field and how do I fill it in?

The slug is the URL address for your article. It must use only lowercase letters, numbers and hyphens — no spaces, no accents, no punctuation. For example, an article called "Acupuncture & Chronic Pain" would have the slug acupuncture-and-chronic-pain.

How do I find the newsletter URL for an article?

The pattern is always: https://www.naturalmed-wellness.com/newsletter/YYYY-MM-slug.html where YYYY-MM is the year and month of the article and slug is what you entered in the Slug field.

A subscriber asked to be removed from the newsletter. What do I do?

Go to contacts.google.com, find the person, click the three dots ⋮ and choose "Remove from label" → Newsletter NaturalMed. They will not receive future newsletters.

The yellow bar is still showing in the email I received.

Before copying the newsletter, you must first click the "✓ Hide this bar — ready to copy" button on the newsletter page. Then do Cmd+A and Cmd+C.

I am not sure if the GitHub Action ran successfully.

Go to https://github.com/naturalmed/naturalmed-wellness/actions. A green circle means success. A red circle means something went wrong — contact your developer.
