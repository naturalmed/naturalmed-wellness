Organ of the Season

The Liver in Spring

In Chinese medicine, Spring belongs to the Liver and Gallbladder. The Liver governs the smooth flow of Qi — when it is balanced, we feel calm, creative and decisive. When it is constrained, we experience irritability, headaches and muscular tension.

Common Symptoms This Season

Signs the Liver Qi is constrained

•  Irritability & mood swings
•  Seasonal allergies
•  Muscular tension or pain

•  Eye fatigue or dryness
•  Headaches at the temples
•  Difficulty sleeping deeply

Chinese Dietetics

Recommended foods for Spring

Green vegetables Lemon Ginger Mint tea Spring onions Sprouts

Foods that move and nourish Liver Qi. Avoid heavy, fried or very spicy foods this season.

Practical Tip · Energetic Exercise

3-step breathing to release Liver Qi

Step 1 — Stand or sit comfortably. Close your eyes and place your hands on your lower ribcage (over the Liver area on the right).

Step 2 — Inhale slowly through the nose for 4 counts, feeling the ribcage expand sideways.

Step 3 — Exhale through the mouth with a gentle "shhh" sound for 6 counts. Repeat 6 times each morning for 2 weeks.

Clinical Case · Authority & Utility

"I had not slept well for three years"

Maria, 44, came with chronic insomnia, irritability and a sensation of heat in the evening. In TCM her pattern was Liver Yin deficiency with rising Yang. After 6 acupuncture sessions and an herbal formula to nourish Liver Yin, she reported sleeping 6–7 hours without interruption. The irritability improved within the first two weeks. This case illustrates how the Liver — when nourished in spring — can restore the natural rhythm of sleep and emotional balance.
